We have AI now, but how do we get data to use with it in order to solve problems like global warming or to improve on certain fields(agriculture, smart city, smart home etc.) IoT or internet of things is something that evolves day by day in order to improve or optimize our life. In order to benefit from IoT we need to get, process and store data. How do we do that? Do you see yourself taking data from a device on an SD card or through NFC? We need wireless communications(like Wi-Fi or Bluetooth) but for longer range. You heard about 5G or LTE. There are solutions for IoT like NB-IoT or LTE-M that are not available to the public. 5G is not something that will come to all regions soon. Would you like to pay monthly for your device subscription? Not all service providers give permission to users or have the needed SIM card as their band costs a lot and not many will use this feature. If you want 1000 SIM-s or E-SIMs, sure but for one or two price is available as "call for the price". You don't have coverage, try to convince your provider to solve it and you know that there will be no change as the coverage is already fixed and optimized. 


          Like 2G what will happen when support is dropped? You need to change the device.

At least for now I see LoRa-WAN as a solution for all of those problems. What I see now, at least in Europe: 


Advantages:

  • low power consumption
  • good signal penetration(compared to the 2.4GHz band for Sigfox, Wi-Fi, BLE or even LoRa in the 2.4GHz band)
  • stable regulation and maturity(available gateways and node devices)
  • free to use(TTN or Chirpstack)
  • available tools for coverage mapping
  • coverage can be improved(bring your gateway for you and your community) with less than 200 euros and everybody can add their device, not to mention the small power consumption of those devices, including the gateway
