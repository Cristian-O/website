We have AI now, but how do we get data to use with it in order to solve problems like global warming or to improve on certain fields(agriculture, smart city, smart home etc.) IoT or internet of things is something that evolves day by day in order to improve or optimize our life. In order to benefit from IoT we need to get, process and store data. How do we do that? Do you see yourself taking data from a device on an SD card or through NFC? We need wireless communications(like Wi-Fi or Bluetooth) but for longer range. You heard about 5G or LTE. There are solutions for IoT like NB-IoT or LTE-M that are not available to the public. 5G is not something that will come to all regions soon. Would you like to pay monthly for your device subscription? Not all service providers give permission to users or have the needed SIM card as their band costs a lot and not many will use this feature. If you want 1000 SIM-s or E-SIMs, sure but for one or two price is available as "call for the price". You don't have coverage, try to convince your provider to solve it and you know that there will be no change as the coverage is already fixed and optimized. 


          Like 2G what will happen when support is dropped? You need to change the device.

At least for now I see LoRa-WAN as a solution for all of those problems. What I see now, at least in Europe: 

Advantages:

  • low power consumption
  • good signal penetration(compared to the 2.4GHz band for Sigfox, Wi-Fi, BLE or even LoRa in the 2.4GHz band)
  • stable regulation and maturity(available gateways and node devices)
  • free to use(TTN or Chirpstack)
  • available tools for coverage mapping
  • coverage can be improved(bring your gateway for you and your community) with less than 200 euros and everybody can add their device, not to mention the small power consumption of those devices, including the gateway


          This is the coverage of a raspberry pi built gateway placed indoor. As you can see it covers almost the entire city not to mention that a better antenna(collinear antenna) will cover for sure the entire city if placed outside. Previous image of the city Suceava shows that a range of 2km can be achieved in a dense city between multiple buildings with a good antenna placed properly.

Challenges with IoT and problems:

  • battery end of life management(mainly recycling, up to 50% of batteries are not disposed properly)
  • battery degradation over time(weather or not rechargeable batteries discharge and loose capacity over time)
  • rechargeable batteries charging(batteries have a limited charge cycle count ~1000-3000 times)
  • recharging on cold temperature(lithium based batteries need to be charged over 0 °C or 32°F; JEITA standards for charging implemented on some TI ICs which prevent charging under freezing point in order to prevent dangerous/high level of battery degradation; charging is stopped as the temperature of the battery rises over 40 to 45)

Acknowledgements:

  • IoT devices spend most of the time waiting to send data(in a low consumption mode)
  • Wireless communications use more power than the microcontroller and sensors alone in a really small amount of time(an energy source that can give high a amount of current fast will be really good-preventing the voltage drop which can cause brownout errors or resets)
  • Solar energy is almost everywhere for a couple of hours per day(at least a couple of hours per week)
  • Solar cells are developing rapidly in efficiency and decrease in size
  • Lithium based supercapacitors are cheaper than ever and available on the market
  • Energy harvesting is viable even in IoT as a batery-less solution
  • LoRaWAN is the most power efficient solution for long range low power and consumer friendly solution(as you can easily deploy your gateway or custom device without a fixed subscription; In Europe TTN has the best availability in Netherlands, Germany, UK, Czech Republic, Austria)

Available solutions right now:

  • Supercapacitor storage
  • Solar energy harvesting
  • Efficient microcontrollers, transceivers and sensors with low power/stand by modes

As a solution for faster integration of supercapacitor storage a stacked development board was chosen. 

  1. Bottom board contains the supercapacitor for energy storage, solar panel and power circuitry. Common supercapacitors under 100F have a 8 to 10mm diameter which makes them fit between common Arduino header stacked PCBs when the mounting pins are bent at 90°.
  2. Middle board contains a microcontroller with the transceiver integrated as well as the antenna/connector which can be programmed via UART(cheapest method of programming microcontrollers other than USB for the more expensive ones) having the advantage of serial messages directly.
  3. Top board contains the sensors as well as expansion headers for further development

Why a stacked solution?

If you search for PC/104 or Arduino shield you will see that modular devices were preferred a long time ago in industrial solutions or learning as they can be easily maintained/serviced. As small devices reduced in size this method was removed providing an opportunity to say as this device is small and complex there is no space to make it user serviceable. IoT devices are simpler than a phone/computer and can be manufactured to be user serviceable as simple as LEGO.

 Why we use cheap Chinese devices? They are cheap but do the job well. Why you will buy a more expensive device? For example you buy a DEWALT, Milwaukee, Bosh, Ryobi etc. drill because you trust the brand or you have other batteries from other tools, they have better battery life and you like their features. Why you buy DURACELL rather than cheap no name batteries, because they last longer.

So we like to have devices that last longer or offer a certain feature. Solar harvesting devices with a supercapacitor last longer and offer the the advantage of less maintenance.

What are the disadvantages?

  • higher cost(as you need really efficient energy harvesters)
  • you can not send data as a machine gun(limited due to the band that is used, most processes take a long time to evolve: temperature, pressure, air quality etc.)
  • the sensors that work with this device need to use a low amount of current and work between 2.5 to 3.3V(most of them do, at least most of those that use I2C or SPI work with 1.8 to 3.6V)

Taking account that this device can work for a longer time most disadvantages will not be a real problem.

Now we have a device. What else is needed?



As the gateway is available and the device functional there is a need to see the data. TTN lets the users see real time data and the link status(RSSI and SNR).

We have a way to see live data. Now we need a way to store that and view it.

Options:

  • Arduino Cloud
  • Datacake
  • Thingspeak
  • Node-RED

All the options are free to use for a certain number of devices. Node-RED can be an option for a self-hosted cloud which can store data from other devices and be used in various other ways.
